Combinations

Choosing items when the order does not matter

1. What is a Combination?

A combination is a way of choosing items where the order does NOT matter.

Picking 3 students from a class is the same group no matter the order they are chosen.

This is the key difference:

  • Permutations → order matters
  • Combinations → order does NOT matter

2. Understanding n and r

These letters mean the same thing as they did in permutations.

  • n = total number of items available
  • r = number of items chosen

Example:

If there are 10 students and you choose 3 of them:

n = 10
r = 3

3. Combination Formula

This formula removes all the different orders that would be counted in permutations.

C(n, r) = n! / ( r!(n − r)! )

Don’t panic — calculators do this for you. The important part is knowing when to use it.

4. Worked Example

Example:

How many ways can you choose 2 pizza toppings from 5 options?

  • n = 5
  • r = 2
C(5, 2) = 10

Pepperoni + mushrooms is the same as mushrooms + pepperoni, so order does not matter.

5. Practice Questions

1. How many ways can you choose 3 books from 8?

2. Choose 2 players from a team of 6.

3. How many different groups of 4 can be made from 10 people?
